# Privacy Policy for Rabbit-Hole Blocker

Effective Date: March 24, 2026

Rabbit-Hole Blocker is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how our browser extension handles your information.

## 1. No Data Collection
Rabbit-Hole Blocker does **not** collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information (PII). We do not have access to your browsing history, email address, IP address, or any other personal data.

## 2. Local Storage
All information generated or used by the extension is stored strictly on your local device. This includes:
*   **User Preferences:** Settings for which website elements to block.
*   **Focus Stats:** Information regarding your focus timer and daily streaks.
*   **Break Mode Status:** Timer data related to scheduled breaks.

This data is stored using the `chrome.storage.local` API and never leaves your computer.

## 3. Use of Permissions
To function effectively, Rabbit-Hole Blocker requires the following permissions, used solely for the purposes described:
*   **activeTab:** Used to identify the current site when the popup is opened to provide site-specific controls.
*   **scripting:** Used to inject CSS styles to hide distracting elements on YouTube and Reddit.
*   **storage:** Used to save your focus statistics and configuration locally on your device.
*   **alarms:** Used to handle the break mode timer and daily streak resets.

## 4. Third-Party Disclosure
Since we do not collect any data, we do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer any information to outside parties.

## 5. Changes to This Policy
We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by the "Effective Date" at the top of this document.

## 6. Contact Us
If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, you may contact the developer through the GitHub repository issues page.
